Teaching Assistant — Drexel University

I’ve supported introductory and conceptual physics courses through recitations, computational and hands‑on laboratories, and student mentoring. Below are the courses I’ve worked on, responsibilities, and selected materials.

Courses & Roles

PHYS 102 — Fundamentals of Physics II (Recitation & Lab, Fall 2024)

  • Led problem‑solving recitations covering electrostatics, capacitors, DC circuits, conductors/semiconductors, magnetism, and electromagnetic induction.
  • Prepared and delivered pre‑lab mini‑lectures; demonstrated experiments; supervised lab work; graded reports.

PHYS 101 — Fundamentals of Physics I (Recitation, Spring 2023)

  • Facilitated recitations on mechanics: kinematics, Newton’s laws, forces, energy, and momentum.
  • Emphasized structured problem‑solving strategies to build conceptual understanding.

PHYS 170 — Electricity and Motion (Recitation, Fall 2022)

  • Taught physics fundamentals to non‑science majors, connecting mechanics and electricity to real‑world applications.
  • Fostered a collaborative, discussion‑first classroom environment.

PHYS 171 — Electricity and Motion (Computational Lab, Fall 2022 & 2023)

  • Guided students through simulations on Newton’s laws, energy conservation, Coulomb’s law, and basic circuits.

PHYS 176 — Light and Sound (Computational Lab, Winter 2022)

  • Introduced optics and wave phenomena using conceptual simulations and guided exploration.
