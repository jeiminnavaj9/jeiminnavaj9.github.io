About Me

I am a Ph.D. candidate in Physics at Drexel University. My academic journey is driven by a deep interest in understanding the origin and inner workings of our universe. Under the mentorship of Dr. David Goldberg, I am focusing on research related to large-scale cosmic structures, specifically through the study of weak gravitational lensing.

My work aims to contribute to the broader understanding of how matter is distributed in the universe and its role in the formation and evolution of galaxies. I am committed to advancing our knowledge in this area and look forward to contributing meaningful insights to the field of cosmology.

Outside of traditional research methods like pen-and-paper and populating chalkboards with equations, I enjoy coding and experimenting with tools like ChatGPT to develop mobile apps and websites. This allows me to explore my interest in technology and stay engaged with creative and technical challenges beyond my academic work.
