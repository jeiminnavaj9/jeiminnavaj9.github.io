About Me

I'm Jeimin Garibnavajwala — a physicist by training and a problem solver at heart. My academic journey through physics research, particularly in cosmology and weak gravitational lensing, has equipped me with strong analytical, programming, and data-driven thinking skills.

After several years of research and teaching at Drexel University, I decided to transition from my Ph.D. program to pursue a full-time career where I can apply my technical expertise and curiosity to real-world problems. I have extensive experience with Python, data analysis, and scientific computation, and I'm passionate about using these skills to draw insights from data and develop solutions that make an impact. My goal is to work in an environment where analytical reasoning, coding, and innovation intersect—whether that's in data science, research analysis, or technology development.

Beyond my professional interests, I enjoy exploring the deeper questions of physics and the universe, learning new tools for data-driven exploration, and finding creative ways to connect science with everyday understanding.
